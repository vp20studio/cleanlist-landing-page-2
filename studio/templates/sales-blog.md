# Sales Blog Template

Use this template for content targeting sales teams, SDRs, and revenue leaders.

## Frontmatter

```yaml
---
title: "[Number/Action] + [Benefit for Sales]"
description: "[150-160 chars: Problem + Solution + Outcome]"
date: "YYYY-MM-DD"
category: "sales"
tags: ["sales", "SDR", "outbound", "relevant-topic"]
author:
  name: "Cleanlist Team"
  role: "Content Team"
featuredImage:
  src: "/images/blog/sales-topic.png"
  alt: "Descriptive alt text"
keywords: ["primary keyword", "secondary keyword", "LSI keyword"]
---
```

## Structure

### 1. The Hook (100-150 words)
- Start with a relatable pain point
- Use "you" language
- Include a stat or surprising fact

Example:
> "Your SDRs sent 500 emails last week. 150 bounced. That's not just bad dataâ€”that's 30% of their time wasted and your domain reputation taking hits. Here's how top-performing teams fix this."

### 2. The Problem (200-250 words)
- Expand on the pain point
- Use specific scenarios
- Include costs (time, money, reputation)
- Make it feel urgent

### 3. The Solution Framework (400-500 words)
- Present 3-5 actionable steps
- Each step gets its own H3
- Include practical examples
- Link to relevant tools/features

#### Step Structure:
```
### [Step Number]: [Action-Oriented Title]

[2-3 sentences explaining the step]

**Example**: [Specific example of this in action]

**Pro tip**: [Advanced insight for this step]
```

### 4. Results Section (150-200 words)
- Include metrics/outcomes
- Customer quote if available
- Before/after comparison

### 5. Quick Wins (100-150 words)
- 3-5 bullet points
- Things they can do today
- Low effort, high impact

### 6. CTA Section (50-75 words)
- Clear next step
- Link to free trial or demo
- Urgency element

---

## Word Count Target: 1,200-1,800 words

## Internal Links to Include:
- Link to relevant product page (waterfall, verification, etc.)
- Link to use case page (/use-cases/sales-teams)
- Link to related blog post
- Link to pricing

## SEO Checklist:
- [ ] Primary keyword in H1 and first 100 words
- [ ] Secondary keywords in H2s
- [ ] "Sales" or "SDR" in meta description
- [ ] Action-oriented title
- [ ] Stats/numbers in content

## Tone Reminders:
- Write like talking to a peer, not lecturing
- Use "you" and "your team" frequently
- Be specific (not "improve results" but "cut bounce rate by 40%")
- Acknowledge their expertise while adding value
